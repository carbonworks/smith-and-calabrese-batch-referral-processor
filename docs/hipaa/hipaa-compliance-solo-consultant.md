# HIPAA Compliance for Solo Technology Consultants

Research into HIPAA certification, training, and compliance requirements for a solopreneur technology consultant handling PHI from mental health practices.

## Key Finding: No Official HIPAA Certification Exists

HHS does not issue, endorse, or recognize any HIPAA certification. Per HHS.gov: "HHS does not endorse or otherwise recognize private organizations' 'certifications' regarding the HIPAA Privacy Rule." Every "HIPAA certified" product on the market is private/third-party.

**Terminology**:
- **Certificate of completion** = finished a training course (like a CPR card)
- **Professional certification** (CHPS, Security+) = met eligibility, passed proctored exam, maintain CEUs
- **Organizational compliance** = documented policies, risk assessments, BAAs, safeguards — what OCR actually evaluates

## Business Associate Status

As a tech consultant who creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of a covered entity, Carbon Works is a **Business Associate** under HIPAA. Since HITECH Act, BAs are **directly liable** for violations — OCR can fine Carbon Works directly.

### Mandatory Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **BAA with each client** | Written agreement before receiving any PHI. Use HHS sample provisions as template. |
| **Security Rule compliance** | Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for ePHI |
| **Breach notification** | Report incidents to covered entity without unreasonable delay (proposed: 24 hours) |
| **Risk assessment** | Document annually + after any tech change. HHS SRA Tool (free, 156 questions) |
| **Policy documentation** | Written policies for PHI handling; retain minimum 6 years |
| **Workforce training** | Document completion (even if solo) |

## What Mental Health Practices Look For (Priority Order)

1. Will you sign a BAA? (non-negotiable)
2. Clear, confident answers about PHI protection
3. Training certificate on file
4. Documented policies (shareable excerpts)
5. Compliance badge on website
6. Professional certification (nice-to-have)
7. Cyber liability insurance (sophisticated clients ask)

## Incoming Regulatory Changes

Proposed HIPAA Security Rule update (NPRM Jan 2025, expected finalization ~May 2026):
- Encryption at rest and in transit becomes **mandatory** (not "addressable")
- MFA becomes **mandatory**
- Breach reporting within **24 hours**
- Annual compliance audits
- Network segmentation requirements

## Recommended Action Plan

### Immediate (~$50, ~12 hours)
1. Complete HHS free training materials
2. Complete HIPAA BA training course (HIPAATraining.com, $30-50)
3. Run HHS SRA Tool (free, 156 questions)
4. Verify BAA provisions in S&C contract against HHS sample
5. Document existing safeguards

### Medium-term (~$150/mo, 1-3 months)
- Subscribe to Accountable HQ Essential ($149/mo)
- Write formal policies using templates
- Set up annual compliance calendar

### Long-term
- CHPS from AHIMA (if deepening into healthcare) or CompTIA Security+ (if staying general tech)
- Cyber liability insurance ($500-2K/year)
